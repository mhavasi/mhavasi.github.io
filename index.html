Bio

I am currently a PhD student in the Computational Biological Learning Group at University of Cambridge (started in 2017) under the supervision of Dr. José Miguel Hernández-Lobato.

My current research focuses on Bayesian deep learning and its applications. I work on topics such as Bayesian neural networks, continual learning, variational inference and their connections to information theory and compression algorithms.

Past:

I was born and raised in Budapest, Hungary. I did my undergraduate studies in Computer Science at University of Cambridge (2013-2016) followed by an MPhil in Machine Learning, Speech and Language Technology at University of Cambridge (2016-2017).

During my undergrad, I did two software engineering internships at Facebook, 2015 in Menlo Park, CA and 2016 in London UK.

In 2019, I spent the summer at Google Brain (Cambridge, MA) working on Bayesian neural networks with my host Jasper Snoek and my co-host Dustin Tran.



Publication highlights

Refining the variational posterior using through iterative optimization, Under review

We prospose an algorithm for improving the expressivity of mean-field variational inference in Bayesian neural networks. Joint work with Jasper Snoek, Dustin Tran, Jonathan Gordon and José Miguel Hernández-Lobato. PDF

Minimal Random Code Learning: Getting Bits Back from Compressed Model Parameters, ICLR 2019

We propose a non-deterministic compression method for neural networks that samples from a variational distribution. Joint work with Robert Peharz and José Miguel Hernández-Lobato. Arxiv

Inference in Deep Gaussian Processes using Stochastic Gradient Hamiltonian Monte Carlo, NeurIPS 2018

We applied an MCMC sampling method to do inference in deep Gaussian processes. Joint work with José Miguel Hernández-Lobato and Juan José Murillo Fuentes. Arxiv
