About Me

I am currently a PhD student in the Computational Biological Learning Group at University of Cambridge (started in 2017) under the supervision of Dr. José Miguel Hernández-Lobato.

I was born and raised in Budapest, Hungary. I did my undergraduate studies in Computer Science at University of Cambridge (2013-2016) followed by an MPhil in Machine Learning, Speech and Language Technology at University of Cambridge (2016-2017). During my undergrad, I did two software engineering internships at Facebook, 2015 in Menlo Park, CA and 2016 in London UK.

My current research focuses on Bayesian optimization, Gaussian processes and compact neural networks. For a complete list of publications, check my Google scholar page.



Publication Highlights

Inference in Deep Gaussian Processes using Stochastic Gradient Hamiltonian Monte Carlo, NIPS 2018

We applied an MCMC sampling method to do inference in deep Gaussian processes. Joint work with José Miguel Hernández-Lobato and Juan José Murillo Fuentes. Arxiv

Minimal Random Code Learning: Getting Bits Back from Compressed Model Parameters, Under review for ICLR 2019

We propose a non-deterministic compression method for neural networks that samples from a variational distribution. Joint work with Robert Peharz and José Miguel Hernández-Lobato. Arxiv
